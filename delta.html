Research grants

Funding for rigorous research into how violence is prevented — and what actually makes nonviolence work in the real world.

What we fund

We fund research that helps the field understand violence and how to reduce it — from field studies and evaluations to theory, measurement and method. We commit for the long term rather than a single cycle, so researchers can follow the question wherever it leads, and grantees keep their freedom, including the freedom to publish openly.

Funding can go to the research itself — salaries, fieldwork, data collection, analysis and the time it takes to think — or to collaborations that connect scholars with the practitioners doing the work. We try to fund what actually advances understanding, not what happens to fit a rigid template.

We know important questions are often carried by early-career and independent researchers with the least support. There is no minimum track record and no requirement to sit inside a large institution — what matters is that the question genuinely advances nonviolence, and that you can describe how your work answers it.

Applications are open on a rolling basis. Clear conditions, a clear process, and decisions explained — every answer, yes or no, comes with reasons. Professionals talking with professionals.
