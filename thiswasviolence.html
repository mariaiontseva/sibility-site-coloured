What this is

#ThisWasViolence is an open campaign for putting words to the moments that get brushed off as normal — a raised hand, a threat dressed up as a joke, a boundary quietly crossed. Naming them is the first step to no longer carrying them alone.

So much harm is never counted, because it is never called what it is. When one person says “this was violence,” it gives the next person permission to recognise their own story — and to know they did not imagine it.

Every account shared adds to a picture the world usually looks away from. Together they show how ordinary this harm is, how patterned, and how much of it hides in the places we are told are safe.

You do not need the perfect words, or proof, or a tidy ending. If it hurt you, it counts. Share as much or as little as you want — in your own voice, on your own terms.
